The foundational dataset (start here):
Financial PhraseBank — This is the single most widely used dataset in the field. It consists of ~4,840 sentences from English-language financial news, each classified as positive, negative, or neutral from the viewpoint of an investor Hugging Face. Almost every financial sentiment model (FinBERT, FinancialBERT, FinGPT, FinLlama) was trained or evaluated on this. It comes in four configurations based on annotator agreement: 50%, 66%, 75%, and 100% agreement, giving you 4,846 down to 2,264 samples respectively Hugging Face. Load it in one line: load_dataset("takala/financial_phrasebank", "sentences_allagree").

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/takala/financial_phrasebank
Paper: Malo et al. (2014), "Good Debt or Bad Debt: Detecting Semantic Orientations in Economic Texts"


HuggingFace datasets (one-line loading):
FiQA Sentiment (Financial Question Answering) — Consists of 17k sentences from microblog headlines and financial news, labeled positive/negative/neutral Hugging Face. A subset of 961 samples is available on HuggingFace with three-class annotations arXiv. This is the standard benchmark dataset alongside Financial PhraseBank.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/pauri32/fiqa-2018

Twitter Financial News Sentiment (TFNS) — An English-language dataset containing an annotated corpus of finance-related tweets with 11,932 documents annotated as Bearish, Bullish, or Neutral Hugging Face.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/zeroshot/twitter-financial-news-sentiment

Financial Tweets Sentiment (mega-collection) — A comprehensive collection merging multiple financial tweet datasets including FIQA Sentiment Classification, Stock Market Tweets, Stock Related Tweet Sentiment, Twitter Stock Sentiment, Crypto Sentiment, and more Hugging Face. This is great because it combines many smaller datasets into one download.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/TimKoornstra/financial-tweets-sentiment

FinGPT Sentiment Train — This is what FinGPT actually trains on. It's an instruction-tuned dataset that reformats multiple source datasets (Financial PhraseBank, FiQA, TFNS, and others) into prompt-response pairs for LLM finetuning.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/FinGPT/fingpt-sentiment-train

FinGPT Forecaster Dataset — Goes beyond sentiment — includes Dow 30 stock data with news for price movement prediction.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/FinGPT/fingpt-forecaster-dow30-202305-202405

News With GPT Instructions (NWGI) — A dataset whose labels were generated by ChatGPT, with 16.2k train samples and 4.05k test samples, containing seven fine-grained classification labels ranging from "strong negative" through "neutral" to "strong positive" Hugging Face. Useful if you want more granular sentiment than just three classes.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/oliverwang15/news_with_gpt_instructions

Sentiment Analysis for Financial News — A simpler version of Financial PhraseBank reformatted for easier use. Contains financial news headlines categorized by sentiment, derived from the Malo et al. (2014) research Hugging Face.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/mltrev23/financial-sentiment-analysis

FinMarBa (Financial Market-Based) — A newer dataset that avoids human annotation biases. Contains 61,252 annotated headlines from 2010 to 2024, with sentiment labels derived from actual market reactions rather than human annotators arXiv. This is significant because traditional datasets label based on what humans think the sentiment is, while this labels based on what the market actually did.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/baptle/financial_headlines_market_based
Paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.22932


Kaggle datasets (download as CSV, easy to use with pandas):
Sentiment Analysis for Financial News (Kaggle) — The most downloaded financial sentiment dataset on Kaggle. Same underlying data as Financial PhraseBank but in a flat CSV format that's trivial to load.

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ankurzing/sentiment-analysis-for-financial-news

Stock News Sentiment Analysis (Large) — A larger dataset of financial news with sentiment labels, commonly used for training.

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/avishek04/stock-news-sentiment-analysislarge

Financial News with Ticker-Level Sentiment — A dataset of 5,530 processed articles with per-company sentiment analysis arXiv, useful for mapping sentiment to specific tickers.

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rdolphin/financial-news-with-ticker-level-sentiment


Twitter/social media specific:
TweetEval — A benchmark consisting of seven heterogeneous tweet classification tasks including sentiment, with fixed train/validation/test splits Hugging Face. Not finance-specific, but useful for general social media sentiment pretraining before domain adaptation.

HuggingFace: https://huggingface.co/datasets/cardiffnlp/tweet_eval

StockTwits data — Not a single dataset but a continuous data source. The FinNLP library can pull StockTwits posts by ticker in real-time. Posts are often self-labeled as bullish/bearish by users, giving you free sentiment labels. You'd build your own dataset by collecting over time.

Access via FinNLP: https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinNLP


SEC filings and earnings calls:
FNSPID (Financial News and Stock Price Integration Dataset) — A massive dataset with 15 million time-aligned financial news records for Nasdaq companies from 1999 to 2023. Used in the FinRL Contest 2025.

Paper reference: used in https://open-finance-lab.github.io/FinRL_Contest_2025/

SEC EDGAR filings — Not pre-labeled for sentiment, but Finnhub's API provides sentiment similarity scores for 10-K and 10-Q filings. You can also pull raw filing text from the SEC EDGAR API (https://efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index?q=) and label it yourself or use your finetuned model.
